# Research Notes

## Product Page UX

Baymard's product page research emphasizes that apparel buyers need confidence before purchase. For Ugly Actions, that means visible size options, clear fit notes, human/in-scale imagery slots, and shipping/returns information near the buying area.

Source: https://baymard.com/blog/current-state-ecommerce-product-page-ux

## Apparel UX

Apparel ecommerce has a specific trust problem: buyers cannot touch or try on the item. The site must compensate with fit, sizing, material, and product photography details.

Source: https://baymard.com/blog/apparel-5-best-practices

## Architecture

Next.js supports fast React storefronts with file-based routing, strong rendering options, and a mature deployment path. Vercel's commerce template validates the general direction of Next.js plus commerce-provider integration.

Sources:
- https://nextjs.org/docs/architecture
- https://vercel.com/templates/nextjs/nextjs-commerce

## Accessibility

Use WCAG AA contrast as the baseline: normal text should reach 4.5:1, and large text/UI graphics should reach 3:1.

Source: https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/Accessibility/Guides/Understanding_WCAG/Perceivable/Color_contrast
